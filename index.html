The Greeks

Understand how options are priced

Δ
Delta
Rate of change of option price vs. underlying price. Ranges -1 to +1. Also approximates probability of expiring ITM.
Stock moves +$1Δ = 0.50
A 0.50 delta call gains ~$50 per contract when the stock rises $1
Θ
Theta
Time decay. How much value the option loses each day. Works FOR sellers, AGAINST buyers. Accelerates near expiration.
Days to expiry30 DTE
At 30 DTE, theta decay is moderate. Premium erodes roughly linearly until ~21 DTE, then accelerates.
Γ
Gamma
Rate of change of delta. Highest for ATM options near expiration. High gamma = position changes rapidly as stock moves.
MoneynessATM
ATM options have highest gamma — your delta changes fastest. This is why 0DTE positions move explosively.
V
Vega
Sensitivity to implied volatility changes. Buyers want IV to rise (long vega). Sellers want IV to fall (short vega).
IV change+5 vol pts
A +5 vol point IV increase adds ~$25 to a vega-0.05 option per contract. Pre-earnings IV expansion benefits long options.
ρ
Rho
Sensitivity to interest rate changes. Least impactful of the main greeks for short-term options. More relevant for LEAPS.
Calls have positive rho (benefit from rate hikes). Puts have negative rho. For most retail traders, rho is negligible unless trading LEAPS or in a rapidly changing rate environment.
